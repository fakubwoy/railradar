Reading the movement chart

The chart plots WL position over time — lower is better (closer to confirmation). A steep downward slope is good. Flat line means no cancellations on your quota. Key cancellation windows: T-120h → T-72h → T-24h → T-4h (chart prep). If GNWL and moving 3+ positions/day within 5 days of departure, odds are good. PQWL with no movement = book an alternative.

Quota & Waitlist Guide
India's train reservation system decoded — quotas, waitlists, the longer-route trick, and timing strategies.

How confirmation works

When a seat is booked, the ticket enters a quota-specific waitlist. Cancellations only free up seats within the same quota — a GNWL cancellation never moves a PQWL ticket up. This is why GNWL 40 often confirms before PQWL 5. Priority order: CNF > RAC > GNWL > RLWL > PQWL > TQWL > RQWL.

The longer-route trick: PQWL → GNWL

If you travel HYD → Katpadi on a train from Hyderabad to Chennai: booking HYD → Chennai gives you GNWL (origin quota, huge pool). Booking HYD → Katpadi gives PQWL (tiny shared pool). You can deboard at Katpadi — the ticket is valid. Extra cost: ₹80–250. Confirmation probability: roughly doubles.

Tatkal & Premium Tatkal

Tatkal opens 1 day before departure (AC classes) or 1 day before (SL). Premium Tatkal is dynamic pricing — prices increase as seats fill. Both have no WL — if the seat isn't available at the time of booking, it's a flat REGRET. For same-day travel, always check Tatkal first. Tatkal charges: SL ₹100–200 extra, AC ₹300–500+ extra.

Cancellation windows to watch

T-120h: Passengers with backup bookings cancel — good GNWL movement.
T-72h: Flights/buses finalized — major cancellation wave.
T-24h: Last-minute plan changes.
T-4h: Chart preparation — VIP, defence, foreign tourist quota released back. GNWL can jump significantly.

Hyderabad: three stations, three quotas

SC (Secunderabad), HYB (Hyderabad Deccan), and KCG (Kacheguda) each anchor different trains with independent quota allocations. If SC shows PQWL, check HYB or KCG — the quota type can be entirely different on trains originating from there.
